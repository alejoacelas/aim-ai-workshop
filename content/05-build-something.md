# Build something useful

You now have a coding agent, unrestricted permissions, Google Workspace connected, and a set of skills. Now that you're connected to your Drive, you can take anything there and have your agent work on it.

Use `/plan` to think through your idea before building. Describe what you want, and your agent will check feasibility, identify setup steps, and break it into a plan—without writing any code yet.

:::prompt
/plan [describe what you want to build in a sentence or two]
:::

Here are some directions you could go:

- **Work hub** — Have your agent explore a Drive folder and build a summary of your project's current state, goals, and where key information lives.
- **Dashboard from a spreadsheet** — Turn a Google Sheet into a clean, shareable webpage with charts and filters.
- **Weekly reporting** — Pull data from a Sheet, write a summary, and email it to your board every Friday.
- **Email triage** — Read your inbox every morning, categorize messages by urgency, draft replies for routine requests.
- **Meeting prep** — Given a calendar event, search your Drive for relevant documents and summarize them in a briefing.
- **Donor acknowledgment** — When a new row appears in a Sheet, draft a personalized thank-you email.
- **Impact report** — Pull program data from Sheets, combine with template text, and generate a formatted PDF.
- **Grant tracking** — Read a spreadsheet of grant deadlines, format it into a clean status view you can share with your team.
- **Communication tools** — Draft outreach emails, newsletter content, or partner updates using context from your Drive.
- **Research and writing** — Search the web, summarize findings, and draft a memo or brief on a topic you're exploring.
